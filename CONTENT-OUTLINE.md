# Errorfect Content Outline

## Initial Blog Posts (10 Core Words)

### 1. Normal → Or/Mal
- Etymology: Latin *norma* (carpenter's square)
- Deconstruction: "either bad" when N is removed
- How it operates: binary + moralized
- Reframe: "I don't match a narrow measuring tool someone invented"

### 2. Perfect → Error-Fect
- Etymology: Latin *perfectus* (thoroughly made, finished)
- Deconstruction: "error effect" when P is removed
- How it operates: pretends to be absence of error
- Reframe: "A finished state that forbids error forbids growth"

### 3. Control → Counter-Roll
- Etymology: French *contrerôle* (duplicate register)
- Deconstruction: verification, not domination
- How it operates: constant audit against ideal
- Reframe: "I can influence probabilities, not guarantee outcomes"

### 4. Error → Wandering
- Etymology: Latin *errare* (to wander, stray)
- Deconstruction: movement off a path, not shameful
- How it operates: demonized in straight-line culture
- Reframe: "Exploratory data point with a price"

### 5. Sin → Missing the Mark
- Etymology: Greek *hamartia* (archer missing target)
- Deconstruction: fixable action, not identity condemnation
- How it operates: shifted from action to essence
- Reframe: "I mis-aimed, which is correctable and human"

### 6. Crazy → Cracked
- Etymology: related to "crack, crush, shatter"
- Deconstruction: structural damage, not soul pathology
- How it operates: collapses all difference into "broken"
- Reframe: "Cracks in the default story are often openings"

### 7. Natural → What Arises
- Etymology: Latin *natura* (birth, character)
- Deconstruction: what arises by itself, not "morally good"
- How it operates: pretends to be descriptive, arrives with "ought"
- Reframe: "This matches my preferred myth about how things should arise"

### 8. Healthy → Whole
- Etymology: Old English *hælþ* (wholeness, soundness)
- Deconstruction: intactness, not thin/productive/compliant
- How it operates: swapped to aesthetics and labor demands
- Reframe: "Wholeness, not compliance with specific aesthetics"

### 9. Deserve → Serve Well
- Etymology: Latin *deservire* (serve well, devote)
- Deconstruction: ledger of service to something
- How it operates: value earned by service to god/state/company
- Reframe: "Service to what, exactly? Capitalism? A doctrine? A family script?"

### 10. Success → What Follows
- Etymology: Latin *successus* (outcome, result)
- Deconstruction: what happened, not moral verdict
- How it operates: mutated to socially admired outcome
- Reframe: "This is what happened given the inputs, constraints, and randomness"
